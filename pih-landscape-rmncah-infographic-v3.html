Assessment Question
Sample Answer — PIH Rwanda
Source
1
What proportion of women in this catchment area delivered at a health facility last year?
94% facility delivery rate (consistent with Rwanda national average)
BNA
2
What are the most common reasons women give for not attending ANC or delivering at a facility?
Cost of transport, fear of C-section, preference for traditional birth attendant among older women
BNA
3
Do facility utilization records indicate which villages have the highest and lowest ANC attendance and facility delivery rates?
Yes — ANC registers show catchment villages with consistently high attendance; 2 remote villages lag behind, informing CHW deployment priorities
SAF
4
At what point in pregnancy do women typically first seek ANC in this community?
Median first ANC at 16–18 weeks; Rwanda target is before 12 weeks — gap identified
BNA
5
What proportion of women who had geographic and physical access to ANC did not use it — and what reasons are recorded in exit interview or community survey data?
~8% of women with access did not complete 4 ANC visits; recorded reasons include partner disapproval, work obligations, and preference for TBA for first visit
BNA
6
What is the dropout rate between first ANC visit and fourth ANC visit, and between ANC completion and facility delivery?
First-to-fourth ANC dropout ~12%; ANC-to-facility delivery dropout ~6%; adolescent girls show higher dropout between visits
BNA
7
Are there documented community outreach activities (home visits, community meetings, health fairs) linked to this facility, and are they tracked in registers?
Yes — CHW home visit registers show monthly outreach to all 12 catchment villages; maternity waiting home usage logged at facility level
SAF
8
Is there a formal community health committee linked to this facility? Is it active?
Yes — meets quarterly; includes community and local government representatives
SAF
9
Are there documented external factors — political, economic, or environmental — in district health reports that are affecting health service utilization in this catchment?
Yes — district health reports note periodic population displacement from eastern DRC border; district office tracking impact on immunization coverage
WHO
10
Are there national or district-level policy commitments, new funding streams, or MoH programs specifically targeting demand-side barriers to RMNCAH service use?
Yes — Rwanda GFF co-investment plan includes community demand component; district government increased CHW stipend budget for FY2025–26
